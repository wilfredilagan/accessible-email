Features

Feature: The Thomson Reuters Accessibility Annotations Kit

Accessibility annotations are a hands-on, efficient, and scalable way for designers to communicate needs to engineers. ​The accessibility team has created an accessibility annotations kit for Figma to do this. Within the kit you can find annotations for alt text, headings, alerts, notes, landmarks and reading order and tab order.

Accessibility annotations should be done in Figma after designs are at a point where they are ready for being annotated (Final designs or close to final designs). Accessibility annotations should follow the general template given by the accessibility team.

Documentation and Figma file of kit

Feature: The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) turns 32!

July 26 was National Disability Independence day and it was also the same day that the American with Disabilities Act (ADA) turned 32. This historic civil rights law protects the rights of people with disabilities.

The ADA is a civil rights law that prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in all areas of public life, including jobs, schools, transportation, and all public and private places that are open to the general public. The purpose of the law is to make sure that people with disabilities have the same rights and opportunities as everyone else.

For more information view the ADA Anniversary Toolkit

News

WCAG 2.2 candidate recommendation

On Tuesday August 30, 2022, the WCAG working group agreed to move forward with the WCAG 2.2 candidate recommendation. WCAG is the Web content accessibility guidelines and are the standard which we follow here at Thomson Reuters. It is also the basis of many laws around the world. Members on the Thomson Reuters accessibility team also participate in the accessibility guidelines working group that helps decides on the guidelines themselves.

Accessible prototypes in Figma

Figma announce a prototype screen reader beta that includes support for text notes, alt text for images, and the ability to interact with and navigate through prototypes with buttons and keyboard actions like tabbing.

TR Accessibility testing team assignments

At Thomson Reuters we have a dedicated team for accessibility testing. We also now have dedicated accessibility testers for each design pod. This will allow the accessibility testing be more streamlined with the dedicated accessibility tester. Along with the dedicated testers there are is an new accessibility testing process that the accessibility team has created. This will involve using some of the tooling, Level Access AMP, that will provide an easier way to understand the accessibility bugs that arise
